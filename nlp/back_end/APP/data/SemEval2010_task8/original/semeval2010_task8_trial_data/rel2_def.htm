Relation 2: Instrument-Agency

Definition

Instrument-Agency(X, Y) is true of a sentence S that mentions entities X and Y if and only if:

(1) S, X and Y obey the guidelines at http://docs.google.com/Doc?docid=dfhkmm46_0f63mfvf7&hl=en


(2) the situation described in S entails the fact that X is the instrument (tool) of Y or, equivalently, that Y uses X.

Definition – Restrictions

(a) X is an entity and Y is an implied activity or an explicit actor. That is to say, there exists an activity even if the close context for X and Y includes no verb.

(b) The relation is true if the sentence context implies that Y uses, is using or has used X. (No future use: we exclude modalities.)

Definition – Notes

(i) Examples that illustrate (a):

  • Instrument-Agency(pump, drainage) means "pump is the instrument of drainage (draining)"
  • Instrument-Agency(laser, printer) means "the printer (printing) uses laser"
  • Instrument-Agency(toothpick, digging) means "digging with a toothpick"
  • Instrument-Agency(ax, murderer) means "murderer (murdering) uses ax".


(ii) An example illustrating (b):

  • "The <e1>dentist</e1> inserted the <e2>drill</e2> and began work." implies that the dentist (Agency) is using the drill (Instrument).


Definition - Overlaps:


There are no obvious overlapping relations.

Near-miss Negative Examples

"The gain medium transfers external energy into the <e1>laser</e1> <e2>beam</e2>."
Product-Producer(e1, e2)

Comment: The laser creates the beam, it does not use it.


"Grindex submersible <e1>drainage</e1> <e2>pumps</e2> are used for pumping fluids with abrasive particles."
Other(e1, e2)

Comment: The relation between e1 and e2 is Purpose-Object: this pump is for drainage (draining). This relation is not in our inventory and thus we have an instance of Other.


"This is the simplest, safest <e1>axe</e1> <e2>sharpener</e2>, tool sharpener that we have ever used."
Other(e1, e2)

Comment: The relation between e1 and e2 is Object(Theme)-Agency: axe is the object of sharpening, and "sharpener" is just an intermediary.

The relation between e2 and e1 could be Purpose-Object: this ax is for sharpening (other things).


"The <e1>dentist</e1> lowered the <e2>chair</e2> and began work."
Other(e1, e2)
Comment: The chair is not involved in the activity of the dentist.
